The 1FPS Theater

The year is 2099. You are a projectionist for a retro movie theater: The 1FPS Theater. Owned by a mysterious eccentric, employment at 1FPS is coveted and your terms of work are simple: you can play whatever media you want, provided images are rendered at 1-frame-per-second (FPS).

It's March 31st. 100 years after the Watchowskis released The Matrix and you'd like it to be tonight's attraction. It was originally projected at 24 FPS and had a runtime of 136 minutes. That is 8,160 seconds. That is 195,840 images.

Your job is to reduce the film from 195,840 to 8,160 images. One for every second of runtime. Don't ask why. You like your job.

So how do you cut 96% of the film and still have it render a meaningful story?

Patience!

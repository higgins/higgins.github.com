tl;dr: With Forward Email, gmail's "send mail as" feature and a SAAS SMTP service you can send and receive emails on your custom domain for free.

I don't like paying for things I don't use. Particularly SAAS products.

I've paid for Google's workspace tools for several projects but think I found a better alternative.

The problem: I need a free way to send and receive emails under a domain I own without hosting an email server. I don't care about the other cloud features.

Here is how I did it:

  • To receive emails on a custom domain, sign up for Foward Email.
  • To send emails on a custom domain...
    • Find a hosted SMTP service that is free and within your sending throughput. SendGrid is free but limits daily use. AWS SES offers a free tier then usage based pricing.
    • Configure your personal (free) gmail account to "send as" this domain.

That's it! Now, when I want to send an email on one of my project domains, I select from the "From" dropdown in gmail's composer. Replies are handled by Forward Email. Below are some screenshots you might find useful if you set this up.
